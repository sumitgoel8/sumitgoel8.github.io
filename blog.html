  1. "Redeeming credit card rewards"

    Credit card companies offer many different rewards to their customers for shopping using their credit cards. In this post, we take the case of a simple and popular reward known as the cashback reward. So suppose you have a credit card of company X that gives you cashback of 1% back on all purchases. This means that if you make a purchase of $10000 using this credit card, you'll get a cashback of $100. Now company X offers various ways in which you could redeem this benefit of $100. Let's consider two ways in particular

    • Statement credit : This means that you can simply reduce your outstanding balance by the amount of your reward. So if your balance is $10000 and you redeem the benefit of $100 as statement credit, your outstanding balance becomes $9900.
    • Shopping on amazon : This means that you can directly use your $100 benefit for purchases on Amazon.
    One might be tempted to think that from the customer's point of view, the two ways of redeeming the benefit are equivalent. But in this post, I'll illustrate using an example that it is always better to redeem via statement credit.
    So take the above customer who has a $10000 outstanding balance to the credit card company, $100 worth of rewards and let's say the customer wants to shop for $100 on amazon. Let's see what happens under the two ways discussed above:
    • Statement credit : Here, the customer used the $100 reward for statement credit so the outstanding balance was $9900. So the customer shops using the credit card on amazon for $100 and so it's outstanding balance is $10000 and it also has a cashback reward of $1!
    • Shopping on amazon : In this case, the customer just uses the $100 reward for purchasing on amazon and so it is left with an outstanding balance of $10000 and no rewards.
    So in this example, you save a dollar just by using your rewards as statement credit instead of actual purchases. The main point here is that paying via your card earns you 1% cashback which you're giving up by using your rewards for purchases that can be covered with your card.
